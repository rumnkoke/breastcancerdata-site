The data gap is real

When you're diagnosed with breast cancer, you need information immediately. But the information available is fragmented, clinical, or designed for researchers — not for you.

You might search for what to expect after your specific diagnosis and find medical journals written for oncologists, conflicting advice, or support groups with stories but no data. You're expected to make some of the biggest decisions of your life while processing shock and fear, often without the foundational information you need to feel confident in your choices.

Breast cancer is complex. The information about it shouldn't be.

What we're building

breastcancerdata.org will be a place where patients own their data and can explore real outcomes from people like them — not population averages, but insights from women with similar diagnoses, stages, and treatments.

We're using healthcare data standards (mCODE and FHIR) so that every piece of data contributed has real research value. Your experience won't just help others navigating diagnosis — it will contribute to better understanding of breast cancer outcomes for everyone.

Patient-reported data, accessible visualizations, and a community built on transparency and trust.

How we operate

Standards-aligned

Who this is for

Patients — newly diagnosed or years into your journey, seeking real data about outcomes for people like you.

Caregivers & families — supporting someone you love and looking for clarity in a confusing time.

Survivors — your experience matters. Sharing your data helps the next person facing what you faced.

Researchers — who want patient-centered data that bridges the gap between registries and lived experience.
